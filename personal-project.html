Linear actuator mk-1

My first attempt at designing a 3d printed actuator. This design used a 6v geared motor and ended up being to slow.

Linear actuator mk-2

My second attempt at designing a 3d printed actuator. This design used a 6v geared motor and ended up having too much energy loss due to the plastic gears.

Linear actuator mk-2

My third attempt at designing a 3d printed actuator. This design used a 6v lead scew motor and worked perfectly with no internal gearing.

MDF Panel Cart

This cart was designed to carry multiple 4x8 sheets of material. In addition, it features an offcut storage section as well as a 8ft plank storage section underneath.

Multi-Function Wall Cabinet

This cabinet was designed to carry two small-parts storage bins. In addition, it features a compartment underneath for wiring led lights as well as smart speakers.

Meet The Creator

Jack Herron

Hi, my name is Jack and I live in BC, Canada. When I was growing up I always had a fascination towords how the world works. I always felt the need to crack things open to finally understand them. This curiosity led me towards a coding course I took in grade 9. This course opened my eyes to the possibility of understanding even the most complicated of consumer electronics. From their I began learning simple electronics after i was introduced to arduino microcontrollers. These micro-controllers gave me the ability to visualize my code in a way I wasn't able to before. before long I became interested in 3d design and purchased my first 3d printer, a creality ender 3. With this printer I was able to combine multiple skillsets in order to create what my mind came up with. Now, in order to complete another skillset, I am learning web-design. I hope that this will help me invision new projects that can keep up with the moving world! (the photo is a filler, not me!)
